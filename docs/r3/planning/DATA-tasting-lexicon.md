# DATA — Tasting lexicon (structure & texture)

Transcribed 2026-07-20 for the planning lane (R30/R31, ledger §1 + §4). This is a **different layer**
from the aroma wheel (`DATA-flavour-wheel.md`): it is *structure, body, and texture* — how a tea is
*built* and how it *feels* in the mouth — not what it smells/tastes *of*. That distinction is the
"two-layer question" the ledger §4 entry raises (structure vs aroma; ties to `PHASE2-PRESPEC-NOTES.md`
§F on bitter/strong). Kept separate on purpose so the two layers don't get merged into one vocabulary.

**Source.** Photographed page 223, **"A Brief Lexicon of Tasting"**, of *Tea: History, Terroirs,
Varieties* (Third Edition), Kevin Gascoyne, François Marchand, Jasmin Desharnais & Hugo Américi (Camellia
Sinensis) — the same book as the flavour wheel (see `DATA-flavour-wheel.md` for the full source note;
authors + edition confirmed from the cover 2026-07-21; the p.223 ref is edition-specific; the earlier
Palais des Thés / Delmas lead was wrong and is struck).

**Local-only images.** The source photograph lives in `book_reference_material/` (gitignored, public
repo, copyrighted pages). Only this **word list** is committed. The author's definitions are deliberately
**omitted** — the planning lane will write its own glosses. The words are entry headwords (data); the
definitions are the author's expression.

**Verification.** Page 223 was fully legible; all 43 headwords transcribed verbatim, none `[unclear]`.

## Notes for the planning lane (not from the source)
- This layer is where `astringent` (an R30 orphan just added to `KB_FLAVOR_CHIPS` as vocabulary) actually
  belongs — as a *structural/texture* sensation, not an aroma. Several entries here (astringent · bite ·
  body · full · round · rough · tannic · watery) are candidates for the "structure" dimension the ledger
  §4 entry and `PHASE2-PRESPEC-NOTES.md` §F contemplate: bitter-without-strength → temperature,
  strong-without-bitterness → ratio.
- Do **not** wire these into `isFlavorVocab`/capture/seeds as if they were aromas — that would repeat the
  one-vocabulary-for-two-concepts mistake R30 just fixed. They are a *second* axis, pending the two-layer
  decision on the brewing-session agenda.
